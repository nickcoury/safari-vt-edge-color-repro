View Transition Card

This card acts as a shared element during a View Transition. It starts truncated to ~two lines. Tap anywhere on the page to trigger a crossfade + morph transition that expands the card to reveal the full content.

The View Transitions API captures a snapshot of this card before the DOM update, then crossfades between the old snapshot and the new live state. Because the card has view-transition-name: card set, the browser treats it as a shared element and attempts to morph between the two states — animating position, size, and shape differences.

This is a minimal reproduction for testing Safari on edge-to-edge displays (iPhone with Dynamic Island / notch). The page uses viewport-fit=cover and extends content into the safe areas to surface any color or rendering glitches during the transition animation.

Section One — Setup

This content exists to force the page well beyond a single viewport, ensuring the browser has scrollable content. On a typical phone display (≈800px tall), this page spans roughly 2.5 viewports.

The white background should remain solid through the entire view transition — no flickers, no black flashes, no bleed from underlying layers.

Section Two — Safari View Transitions

Safari shipped support for the View Transitions API (same-document) starting in Safari 18.2. Shared element transitions using view-transition-name should smoothly animate the card between collapsed and expanded states.

On notched iPhones with edge-to-edge displays, the view transition overlay layer may not respect the page background color behind the status bar area, resulting in a visible flash of a different color (often black or the system background).

Section Three — Expected vs. Actual

Expected: Tapping anywhere on the page triggers a smooth view transition. The white page background remains white throughout. The card expands in place with a crossfade/morph animation. No color flashes appear in the safe area regions.

Actual (bug): During the transition, the area behind the notch / Dynamic Island briefly renders in black or dark gray instead of white, creating a jarring flash. This is the edge-to-edge VT color issue this repro targets.

Section Four — Environment

Test on Safari (iOS 18.2+ or latest) on a device with a notch or Dynamic Island — iPhone 14 Pro or later is ideal. The effect is most visible in light mode with a white page background.

Tap the card or anywhere on the page to trigger. Tap again to collapse. Repeat and observe the safe area region above the card during the transition animation.
