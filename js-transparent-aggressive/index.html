Variant 18
JS: Aggressively Transparent During VT Callback. Inside the callback, html, body, AND .page are all set to background: transparent. The entire page is transparent when the new snapshot is captured. Restored after transition.finished.
// Inside startViewTransition callback: document.documentElement.style.background = 'transparent'; document.body.style.background = 'transparent'; pageEl.style.background = 'transparent'; updateCardState(); // After transition.finished: restore all
View Transition Card

Maximum transparency during the new-state snapshot: html, body, and the .page wrapper are all set to transparent in the VT callback. When Safari captures the new snapshot, the entire viewport (including safe areas) has no opaque white background.

This is the most aggressive JS approach — if Safari still shows white in safe areas, the bar color is definitively not derived from the page's computed background.
